# Mood Vocabulary Reference

## Happy
Positive emotional state. Songs feel uplifting, bright, and joyful. High valence (musical positivity). Often in major keys with major chord progressions. Good for celebrations, mornings, and energizing routines.

## Chill
Relaxed, laid-back atmosphere. Mid-to-low energy. Designed to lower stress and create calm. Common in lofi, ambient, downtempo electronic, and acoustic styles.

## Focused
Music designed to support concentration. Minimal lyrics or instrumental. Steady rhythm without sudden changes. Lofi, ambient, and classical work well. Avoids dramatic dynamic shifts.

## Intense
High emotional charge with strong dynamics. Could be aggressive (metal), driving (EDM), or emotionally heavy (cinematic). Best for workouts, gaming, or moments requiring focus and adrenaline.

## Moody
Atmospheric and emotionally complex. Often blends melancholy with beauty. Synthwave, indie, and certain hip hop fall here. Good for introspective moments, late nights, and creative work.

## Nostalgic
Evokes memory and longing. Often uses retro production techniques (vinyl crackle, tape saturation, analog synths). Lofi and synthwave specialize in this mood.

## Euphoric
Peak emotional high. Builds and drops in EDM, soaring choruses in pop. Designed to create intense positive feelings. Best for festivals and peak workout moments.

## Aggressive
Confrontational, hard-hitting energy. Heavy guitars, harsh vocals, fast tempos. Metal and hardcore subgenres specialize here. Cathartic release for stress or anger.
