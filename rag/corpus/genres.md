# Music Genre Reference Guide

## Pop
Mainstream music with catchy hooks, polished production, and broad appeal. Typically 3-4 minute songs with verse-chorus structure. Energy ranges medium to high. Modern pop blends elements of EDM, hip-hop, and R&B. Best for upbeat moods, parties, and casual listening.

## Lofi
Low-fidelity hip-hop characterized by mellow beats, vinyl crackle, jazz samples, and ambient sounds. Designed for focus, study, and relaxation. Tempo usually 70-90 BPM. Strongly associated with productivity and late-night creative work.

## EDM
Electronic Dance Music built around synthesizers, heavy basslines, and four-on-the-floor beats. Subgenres include house, trance, dubstep, and future bass. High energy, designed for clubs and festivals. Best for workouts and high-energy social settings.

## Rock
Guitar-driven music with strong rhythm sections. Ranges from soft alternative to heavy metal. Emphasizes raw emotion, instrumental skill, and lyrical themes of rebellion or introspection. Energy varies widely by subgenre.

## Hip Hop
Rhythmic vocal delivery (rapping) over beats. Emphasizes lyricism, wordplay, and cultural commentary. Production styles range from boom-bap to trap. Energy depends on subgenre — focused for lofi rap, intense for trap.

## Indie Pop
Independent pop music with experimental, quirky, or DIY aesthetics. Often features unconventional song structures and introspective lyrics. Medium energy, melodic, emotionally resonant.

## Synthwave
Retro-futuristic electronic music inspired by 1980s soundtracks and video games. Heavy use of analog synthesizers, slow-to-mid tempo, nostalgic and moody atmosphere. Best for night drives and cinematic moods.

## Metal
Heavy, aggressive rock with distorted guitars, fast drumming, and powerful vocals. High intensity throughout. Subgenres include thrash, death, and progressive metal. Best for high-energy moments requiring catharsis.
